1D-CIRA

\[\begin{aligned} & T_{nudged} = \alpha_{NP} T_{profile} + (1 - \alpha_{NP}) T_{dynamic} \\ & \text{where} \quad \alpha_{NP} = \frac{k \Delta t}{\tau} \end{aligned} \]

- `$\tau$` chosen to be 24hours in keeping with Song et al. (2018) - `$k \in [0,1]$` is a 'nudging ramp parameter'.

"They found that between 8, 24 and 40 h timescales, the 24 h gave the most reasonable model fields and conservation properties out of the three options". Information from Song et al. (2018)

1D-CIRA

`$T_{profile}$` is based on climatological and satellite data:

- 70-86 km is based on the US Standard Atmosphere (USSA) (COESA, 1976). - 86-119.7 km is based on the Committee on Space Research (COSPAR) International Reference Atmosphere (CIRA) - 119.7+ km the temperature asymptotes to a selected exobase temperature (here 1000 K).

"They found that between 8, 24 and 40 h timescales, the 24 h gave the most reasonable model fields and conservation properties out of the three options". Information from Song et al. (2018)

3D-CIRA

\[\begin{aligned} T_m(m, \varphi) & = T' + T_a \cos(|m - 6|\pi/6)\cos(\pi/2 + \varphi) \\ z_m(m, \varphi) & = z' + z_a \cos(|m - 6|\pi/6)\cos(\pi/2 + \varphi) \end{aligned} \]

`$m$` = month, `$\varphi$` = latitude `$\newline$` `$T' = 178.4482K$`, `$T_a = 25.73031K$` `$\newline$` `$z' = 94.066km$`, `$z_a = 4.561km$`

3D-CIRA

\[ T(m, \varphi, z) = T_m(m, \varphi)\Gamma(z - z_m(m, \varphi))^a \]

`$\Gamma = 4.025643\times10^{-6} Kkm^{-1} \newline$` `$a = 2.414574$`
